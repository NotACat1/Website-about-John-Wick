The triumphant return of Keanu Reeves

The triumphant return of Keanu Reeves

At the end of the 2000s, the actor's career turned clearly somewhere in the wrong direction. The star of teen comedies (Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure), festival movies (My Own Private Idaho) and multimillion-dollar blockbusters (The Matrix) has begun appearing predominantly in critically acclaimed projects (The Day the Earth Stood Still) or fail at the box office ("47 Ronin"). The situation was changed by the release of "John Wick" - then the renaissance of Keanu Reeves began, who again became an important action movie hero, everyone's Internet favorite and even successfully integrated into other media, giving the appearance and voice of revolutionary rocker Johnny Silverhand from the "Cyberpunk 2077" video game.

Dizzying stunts

Dizzying stunts

The secret to the success of the franchise is in the impeccable choreography of the fight scenes. It is likely that Derek Kolstad's script could get a rather mediocre incarnation and "John Wick" it would not have become a phenomenon. But Reeves managed to persuade Chad Stahelski to become directors. and David Leitch - before that they were stuntmen and stunt coordinators of the Matrix trilogy, during the work on which they met the actor.

The rich experience and wild imagination of Stahelski and Leitch helped turn John Wick into perfect attraction. Each film in the franchise is remembered for its action scenes. In the first part is a shootout in a nightclub to Kaleida's electropop hit "Think". The brightest the moment of the sequel is a fierce batch at a concert in the Italian catacombs. And the third and best film consists of a series of outstanding fights - from a fight in the library using a volume of Russian fairy tales to a protracted duel in a glass room against the backdrop of eye-catching video art.

Cheerful "beshka"

Cheerful "beshka"

Screenwriter Kolstad clearly knows what he's doing. "John Wick" is a concentrate of everything "cool" that only existed in cinema: from spaghetti westerns with Clint Eastwood to action films by John Woo. At the same time, the action movie with Reeves is full of spreading cranberries: for example, in the third part it turns out that John Wick is ... Belarusian. “My name is Jordani Jovanovich, I am a child of Belarus, an orphan from your tribe,” the main character says with anguish. It is impossible not to smile at this moment. There are also numerous greetings to connoisseurs of the genre. For example, in the second part, Franco Nero, the same Django from the film series of the same name, appears in a small role. And in the third film, the role of the obsessed killer Zero was played by Mark Dacascos, a martial artist and a prominent figure in category B action films (he, for example, played in the fifth Kickboxer and himself as a director directed Showdown in Manila with Alexander Nevsky in the title role).

Thoughtful cinematic universe

Thoughtful cinematic universe

“John Wick” also captivates with the fact that with each new part it tells more about the cunningly arranged world of assassins. In this universe, nothing happens without the international elite organization of the Board of Clans, which brings together crime bosses from different countries. And the roots of the killer organized crime group go somewhere in the days of the Assassins.

The Wick Cinematic Universe is already cramped in the licensed parts of the franchise - and it will expand. In the near future, the spin-off "Ballerina" about a hired killer, played by Ana de Armas, should be released (filming began in November 2022). And in 2023, the mini-series Continental about the legendary New York hotel, a meeting place for professional killers, will be released on Peacock streaming.

John Wick

John Wick

High-profile hitman John Wick (Keanu Reeves), nicknamed Baba Yaga, retires from the profession and lives peacefully until his wife Elena (Bridget Moynahan) dies of a terminal illness.

A week after the tragedy, reckless young gangster Josef Tarasov (Alfie Allen) foolishly breaks into Wick's house. Together with accomplices, he kills his beloved puppy John and steals a car. Ironically, Josef is the son of Russian mobster Viggo (Michael Nykvist), Wick's former boss.

Viggo tries to negotiate with John, but to no avail. Then he hires killers Marcus (Willem Dafoe) and Miss Perkins (Adrianne Palicki) to put an end to Wick, but nothing works either: the first goes over to the side of the protagonist and dies, and the second is executed for violating the rules of the New York Continental Hotel ”, whose owner Winston (Ian McShane) and his right-hand man Charon (Lance Reddick) enforce the rules (murder is prohibited on the territory of the Continental).

It all ends with Wick killing Josef and then Viggo. In the finale, an injured John picks up a lone pit bull from the veterinary clinic.

John Wick 2

John Wick 2

John makes peace with Abram (Peter Stormare), brother of the late Viggo. But he only dreams of peace.

Wick is back in the game, so he is visited by Santino D'Antonio (Riccardo Scamarchio), to whom the main character owes a favor. Santino demands to complete his task, destroying the killer's house along the way. John travels to Italy to deal with Gianna (Claudia Gerini), his employer's sister. After her death, Santino will be able to take a place in the Board of Clans, the elite layer of the entire underworld. Gianna kills herself and John is attacked by her bodyguard Cassian (Common). But the fight ends in a draw.

Wick returns to New York and learns that Santino has declared a hunt for him, setting a fee of $ 7 million. John again faces Cassian and this time wins, but leaves the enemy alive. Then the killer gets to Bowler (Laurence Fishburne), the so-called Pigeon King, the patron saint of the homeless. He receives help in the form of treatment and weapons and is sent to the Continental. John kills D'Antonio on the grounds of the inn, thus violating one of the most important rules of the guild.

Winston gives John an hour reprieve. After that, a new contract for his murder comes into effect - now Wick has received the status of an excommunicado (literally, a renunciate), so all the killers are hunting for his head.

John Wick 3

John Wick 3

The action of the film starts immediately after the end of the second part. Everyone is now hunting for John, so he goes to the theater, where he asks for help from the leader of the Slavic group (Angelica Huston played the nameless heroine). She helps him get to Casablanca safely, where Wick turns to his old friend Sophia (Halle Berry) for help. She promises to set him up with the Elder (Said Tagmaui), the legendary head of the criminal world.

John travels to the desert, where he meets with the supreme assassin. He proposes to remove the excommunicado status from Wick, but then the main character will become a pawn in the hands of the Board of Clans. John agrees, and his first task is to kill Winston; in parallel, the Judge (Asia Kate Dillon), sent by the Board, is working on his elimination. She hires high-profile hitman Zero (Mark Dacascos) and his team.

Wick meets with Winston and changes his mind about killing his friend. For this, the Board declares the New York Continental "secularized" - murder is now allowed on its territory. Charon and John defend the hotel from numerous fighters, and Wick defeats Zero and his students. The next day, the Judge arranges negotiations on the roof of the Continental - Winston manages to persuade her to return the hotel to official status. He shoots John and throws him off the roof.

However, Wick's body goes missing. It turns out that the half-dead hitman was saved by Bowler's subordinate, who wants to go against the Board.
